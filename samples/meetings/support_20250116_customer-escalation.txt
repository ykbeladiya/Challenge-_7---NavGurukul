Support Team Meeting - Customer Escalation Process
Date: January 16, 2025
Time: 2:30 PM

Attendees:
- Emma (Support Manager)
- Frank (Support Agent)
- Grace (Support Agent)
- Henry (Support Agent)

AGENDA:
1. Review escalation procedures
2. Discuss customer communication standards
3. Update SLA guidelines

DISCUSSION:

Escalation Process:
Emma: "Let's clarify our escalation workflow. When should you escalate a ticket?"

Frank: "I think we should escalate when the issue is beyond our expertise or requires engineering intervention."

Grace: "Here's the process I follow:
1. Assess ticket priority - review details and determine if escalation is needed
2. Notify customer - send acknowledgment email within 1 hour
3. Escalate to engineering - create internal ticket and assign to appropriate team"

Customer Communication:
Henry: "We need to improve our response times. What's our SLA?"

Emma: "Our Service Level Agreement commits to:
- Initial response within 1 hour
- Resolution within 24 hours for high priority tickets"

DEFINITIONS:
Escalation: The process of transferring a support ticket to a higher level of expertise.
SLA: Service Level Agreement - committed response and resolution times.

FAQs:
Q: When should I escalate a ticket?
A: Escalate when the issue is beyond your expertise or requires engineering intervention.

Q: What is the response time SLA?
A: Initial response within 1 hour, resolution within 24 hours for high priority.

DECISION:
We will implement automated escalation for tickets open > 48 hours. This ensures no tickets are forgotten and improves customer satisfaction.

ACTION ITEMS:
- Create escalation runbook (Owner: Support Manager, Due: 2025-01-25)
